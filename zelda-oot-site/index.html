The Legend of Zelda:
Ocarina of Time

"The flow of time is always cruel... Its speed seems different for each person, but no one can change it..."

Step into the mystical realm of Hyrule, where a young boy named Link embarks on an epic journey across time itself. Released in 1998 for the Nintendo 64, this masterpiece redefined adventure gaming and set the standard for 3D action-RPGs.

From the tranquil depths of Kokiri Forest to the towering spires of Ganon's Castle, experience a world where magic flows through ancient ocarina melodies and destiny awaits at every turn.

A Legend Forged in Time

The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time stands as one of the most influential video games ever created. Released in November 1998 for the Nintendo 64, it marked the series' bold leap into 3D gaming under the direction of Shigeru Miyamoto and Eiji Aonuma.

The game introduced revolutionary mechanics like Z-targeting, context-sensitive actions, and a dynamic camera system that would become industry standards. But beyond its technical achievements, Ocarina of Time crafted an emotional narrative that resonated with millions—a tale of courage, friendship, and the bittersweet passage of time.

Heroes & Villains

The Epic Journey

CHAPTER ONE

The Boy Without a Fairy

In the tranquil Kokiri Forest, a boy named Link lives among the eternal children, the only one without a fairy companion. When the Great Deku Tree summons him, Link's destiny begins to unfold. He must venture into the tree's hollow to cleanse the ancient curse placed upon it by a wicked man from the desert.

CHAPTER TWO

The Spiritual Stones

Guided by Princess Zelda's vision, Link embarks on a quest to gather the three Spiritual Stones. From the Gorons of Death Mountain to the Zoras of the river, he earns the trust of Hyrule's people and collects the stones needed to open the Door of Time. But Ganondorf's shadow looms ever closer.

CHAPTER THREE

The Master Sword

At the Temple of Time, Link draws the legendary Master Sword, becoming the Hero of Time. But the sword seals him away for seven years, for he was too young to be the hero. When he awakens, Hyrule has fallen to darkness, and he must journey through time to awaken the Sages who can seal Ganondorf away forever.

FINAL CHAPTER

The Final Battle

With all Sages awakened, Link storms Ganon's Castle. In the crumbling ruins, he faces Ganondorf in an epic duel, then confronts the beast Ganon in the void of the collapsing castle. With the power of the Sages and Zelda's magic, the King of Evil is sealed away, and peace returns to Hyrule. But Link's journey through time leaves him with bittersweet memories of a world that moved on without him.

An Enduring Legacy

More than two decades after its release, Ocarina of Time continues to inspire game developers and captivate players. Its influence can be seen in countless action-adventure games, from its lock-on combat system to its approach to storytelling and world-building.

The game has received numerous accolades, including perfect scores from major publications and recognition as one of the greatest games of all time. Its music, composed by Koji Kondo, remains iconic—the Ocarina melodies are instantly recognizable to gamers worldwide.
